SECTION 01

Earthquake Awareness

Earthquake awareness starts with preparation, calm response, and safe recovery. Follow these practical steps for what to do before, during, and after an earthquake.

BEFORE

  • Careful planning is the best defense against disasters.
  • Learn about the dangers of earthquakes in your area.
  • Find out if your building or other important infrastructure needs strengthening. Retrofit if necessary. Prepare your home, school, and workplace.
  • Secure heavy objects to walls or large cabinets.
  • Repair damaged electrical wiring and leaking gas connections.
  • Place heavy and breakable items on lower shelves.
  • Know your exit routes.
  • Prepare an evacuation plan for children, pregnant women, the elderly, and persons with disabilities.
  • Know where to find and how to use fire extinguishers, first-aid kits, alarms, communication tools, and emergency exits.
  • Prepare an emergency supply kit that includes a first-aid kit, food, water, clothing, a blanket, a battery-powered radio, a flashlight with extra batteries, and other necessities.
  • Regularly conduct and participate in earthquake drills.

DURING

  • STAY CALM.
  • Do the DUCK, COVER, AND HOLD.
  • If possible, quickly open the door.
  • Drop down and take cover under a sturdy table and hold on to it. If there is no table, protect your head and neck.
  • Stay away from glass windows, cabinets, shelves, and other heavy objects.
  • Be alert to falling objects. Stay aware of your surroundings.
  • ‚ö† If you are OUTSIDE, go to an open and safe area.
  • Move away from trees, electric posts, and other structures that may collapse or fall.
  • Stay away from slopes and mountains that may experience landslides.
  • If you are near the sea and feel a strong earthquake, quickly move to higher ground.
  • If driving, pull over to the side of the road and stop. Do not attempt to cross bridges, overpasses, or flyovers because they may have been damaged by the earthquake.

SECTION 02

Before During After Guidelines

Earthquakes always strike without warning, so preparedness is the best protection. Knowing what to do before, during, and after an earthquake can lessen the risk of injury, prevent panic, and most importantly, save lives.

Guidelines - Before

This guide provides practical steps to help you stay safe when an earthquake occurs.

  • Practice ‚ÄúDuck, Cover, and Hold‚Äù. This helps condition your body to respond automatically instead of panicking.
  • Save emergency contacts. This allows you to connect to help immediately without wasting time during crisis.
  • Know evacuation routes in your area. This ensures safer exits and helps you avoid dangerous areas where falling debris and collapsing structures may occur.
  • Prepare an emergency kit. This helps you survive independently when services are unavailable. Include essentials such as food, water, and first-aid supplies.

Guidelines - During

  • Remain calm. Staying calm helps you think rationally, avoid panic-driven decisions, and perform safety actions immediately.

If indoors:

  • Duck, Cover, and Hold. Protect yourself from falling objects and debris.
  • Stay inside. During shaking, staying inside is often safer than running outside where debris may fall.

If outdoors:

  • Move to an open area. Go where falling debris and collapsing buildings are less likely; stay low and protect your head while moving.

Guidelines - After

  • Check yourself and others. Look for injuries, including minor cuts or bruises, and treat them quickly to prevent infection.
  • Expect aftershocks. They may happen minutes, days, or weeks later and can further damage weakened structures.
  • Inspect surroundings. Watch for hazards such as gas leaks, fire, or exposed wires that can cause additional danger.
  • Stay alert. Follow official announcements and guidance from local authorities and trusted sources. Avoid unverified information that can cause unnecessary panic.

SECTION 04

Myth vs Fact

MYTH

Small earthquakes mean a big one will not happen soon.

FACT

Small quakes do not prevent larger earthquakes. Stay prepared at all times because damaging events can still occur without warning.

MYTH

Doorways are always the safest place during an earthquake.

FACT

In most modern buildings, the safest action is Duck, Cover, and Hold under sturdy furniture while protecting your head and neck.

MYTH

You should run outside immediately when shaking starts.

FACT

Running during strong shaking can be dangerous. If indoors, stay inside and take cover to avoid falling debris, glass, and collapsing objects.

MYTH

After the first quake ends, the danger is over.

FACT

Aftershocks may follow minutes, days, or weeks later. Continue checking hazards, follow official advisories, and stay ready to evacuate if needed.
